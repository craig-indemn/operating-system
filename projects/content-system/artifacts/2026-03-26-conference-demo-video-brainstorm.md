# Conference Demo Video Brainstorm — InsurtechNY Spring 2026

## Context

Cam requested video demonstrations of actual customer outcomes for the InsurtechNY Spring conference booth at Chelsea Piers. Videos need to run locally on tablets (iPad, Galaxy Tab) and a Windows PC driving the booth screen. Connectivity may be limited.

Cam shared the "Webmap - Four Outcomes Navigation" matrix (PDF attached in Slack) which organizes all Indemn associates by customer type (Agency, Carrier, Distributor) across four outcome categories (Client Retention, Operational Efficiency, Revenue Growth, Strategic Control).

## Decisions

### Two demos, two modalities
- **Voice: FNOL (Intake Associate for Claims)** — policyholder calls to report an auto claim
- **Web: Smart Inbox (Inbox Associate)** — submission emails arrive, AI triages/extracts/drafts/enters into AMS

### Why these two
- **FNOL**: Universal pain (everyone's filed a claim), concrete outcome (claim number issued), carrier/distributor audience fit for the room (305 Solution Providers, 188 Start-ups, 132 Carriers, 83 Investors, 49 Brokers)
- **Smart Inbox**: Craig is actively building this for a customer, real conviction behind it. Different texture — back-office workflow vs customer-facing voice. B2B vs B2C.
- Together they tell the story: "We save you money (FNOL) and we accelerate your operations (Smart Inbox)"

### Production approach
- Produced videos representing the experience faithfully (not recordings of live agents)
- No real FNOL or Smart Inbox agent exists yet — these would be built for customers
- Option 3 (video AI associate avatar) held in back pocket as an upgrade for FNOL if Cam/Kyle like the initial version

### Video associate upgrade path
- Start with Option 2 (phone call with branded transcript/caption overlay)
- Option 3 (AI avatar associate talking to the caller) is the booth-stopper version
- Build Option 2 first, show to Cam/Kyle, upgrade to Option 3 if they want it

## Video 1: FNOL (Intake Associate for Claims)

### Format
- Personal lines auto claim (most relatable to broadest audience)
- Policyholder dials carrier's existing claims phone number
- Phone call UI with branded captions + running transcript panel
- Must be followable WITHOUT sound (noisy conference booth)
- Sound should also work well for when people are close enough to hear
- Completely branded to Indemn's style

### Tone
- Conversational and natural — not overly empathetic (feels performative from AI), not overly efficient (feels robotic)
- The sweet spot: like talking to a smart, competent person

### Storyboard

**Act 1 — The Moment (5s)**
Subtle animation: a car in a parking lot, slight bump graphic. Nothing dramatic.
Text overlay: "2:47 PM. Kroger parking lot. Someone just backed into your car."

**Act 2 — The Call (~90s)**
Phone screen appears. Policyholder dials 1-800-555-0127. Ringing. Picks up after one ring.

> **Associate:** "Hi, this is Acme Insurance, this is Maya. How can I help you?"
>
> **Caller:** "Hey, yeah — someone just backed into my car in a parking lot."
>
> **Associate:** "Got it. Are you okay? Is anyone hurt?"
>
> **Caller:** "No, no, everyone's fine. Just my bumper and taillight."
>
> **Associate:** "Good, glad everyone's safe. Let's get your claim started. Can I get your name?"
>
> **Caller:** "Mike Reynolds."
>
> **Associate:** "Thanks Mike. I pulled up your policy. You've got the 2023 Honda Accord, right?"
>
> **Caller:** "Yeah, that's the one."
>
> **Associate:** "OK. So tell me what happened — you were parked and someone backed into you?"
>
> **Caller:** "Yeah, I was parked, came out, and this guy was backing out and hit my rear end. He stopped, we exchanged info. I've got his license plate and insurance."
>
> **Associate:** "Perfect, we'll want that. What's the other driver's info?"
>
> *Caller provides: name, plate number, their insurance carrier.*
>
> **Associate:** "Got it. And the damage — you said bumper and taillight?"
>
> **Caller:** "Yeah, the bumper's dented pretty good and the taillight is cracked."

> **Caller:** "Wow. OK. Thanks, Maya."

Transition to back-office view. Clean dashboard showing:

Text overlay: "2 minutes. Zero hold time. Zero data entry. Claim resolved before he left the parking lot."

**Act 4 — CTA (5s)**
Indemn branding.
Channel icons appear: phone, web voice, web chat, email, SMS

## Video 2: Smart Inbox (Inbox Associate)

### Format
- Fly-on-the-wall view: watch emails flow through a pipeline
- Zoom-in/zoom-out structure: scale (volume) + depth (one email end-to-end)
- Visual, kinetic, almost mesmerizing — the kind of thing that draws your eye from 10 feet away

### Storyboard

**Act 1 — The Problem (5s)**
Visual: An inbox counter ticking up. 8... 14... 23 unread.
Text overlay: "Monday. 8:00 AM. 23 submissions arrived overnight."

**Act 2 — The Pipeline (70-80s)**

Zoom out: We see a visual pipeline/board. Emails start flowing in from the left.

**Phase 1 — Triage (~15s)**
Emails land and immediately get classified. Color-coded labels appear:
- "New Submission — GL"
- "Quote Ready — WC"
- "Follow-Up — Missing Loss Runs"
- "Renewal — Auto Fleet"
- "Not a Submission — Out of Office Reply"

They sort themselves into lanes. A counter ticks: 23 classified in 47 seconds.

**Phase 2 — Zoom In (~40s)**
Camera zooms into one email: a new GL submission from a broker.

"From: jessica.parker@worthington-insurance.com
Subject: New Submission — Riverside Landscaping LLC
Attachments: ACORD 125.pdf, ACORD 126.pdf"

The system opens the ACORD forms. Data fields start populating:
- Business: Riverside Landscaping LLC
- Revenue: $1.2M
- Employees: 14
- Operations: Commercial landscaping, tree removal
- Requested: GL $1M/$2M

Data flows into AMS system with subtle callout: "Direct AMS integration — no API required"

A gap analysis runs:
- Business info — complete
- Operations — complete
- Loss runs — missing
- Subcontractor schedule — missing
- Requested coverage — complete

Completeness: 6/8 fields.

A draft reply assembles itself:

> "Hi Jessica — thanks for sending over Riverside Landscaping. I've reviewed the ACORD 125 and 126. To move forward, I'll need:
> 1. Three years of loss runs
> 2. Subcontractor schedule with certificates
>
> Everything else looks good. Happy to prioritize this once those come in."

Toggle visible: "Auto-send: ON" — reply sent automatically at 8:01 AM.
(Making it clear this can run fully autonomous or human-in-the-loop.)

**Phase 3 — Zoom Out (~15s)**
Pull back to the full board. While we were watching that one email:
- 4 classified as "Quote Ready" — carrier quotes extracted, broker forwards drafted
- 6 need follow-up — gap analyses complete, info-request drafts ready
- 3 are renewals — flagged with expiration dates
- 2 stale submissions — auto-follow-up drafts queued (7+ days waiting)
- 8 fully processed, zero human touch needed

Counter: "23 emails. 3 minutes. 8 fully resolved. 15 drafts ready for review."

**Act 3 — The Reveal (15s)**
Clean stats view (no fabricated accuracy numbers):
- 23 emails processed in 3 minutes
- 8 fully resolved — data entered into AMS, replies sent, no human needed
- 15 drafts ready for review
- Zero submissions lost. Zero duplicates.
- AMS updated automatically — no API required

Text overlay: "Your inbox used to be a bottleneck. Now it's a pipeline."

**Act 4 — CTA (5s)**
Same pattern as FNOL video.
Channel icons: email, web portal, API
Text: "One associate. Every channel."
CTA: "indemn.ai"

## Production Tool Stack (Final)

| Tool | Role | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Kling 3.0** | Cinematic clips — parking lot scene, email flow macro views, transitions | ~$8/month Pro |
| **Runway Gen-4** | Backup if Kling quality isn't sufficient for specific shots | ~$35/month Pro |
| **LiveKit Composite Egress** | Real voice call recording with custom branded web template | Existing infrastructure |
| **HTML/CSS (blog site)** | Dashboard screens, email detail UI, branded cards, CTA end cards | Built in-house |
| **Descript** | Final assembly, captions, music ducking, transitions, stitching all acts | ~$33/month Business |
| **HeyGen Avatar IV** | Option 3 upgrade — AI avatar "Maya" for FNOL (later, if wanted) | ~$29/month Creator |

**Rejected tools:**
- Higgsfield — tuned for social/selfie content, not cinematic
- Canva — not needed, HTML/CSS covers branded graphics
- Creatify — e-commerce ads, not SaaS product demos
- Pika — too stylized and imprecise
- InVideo — stock footage assembler, can't composite custom sources
- Local video pipeline skills — not maintained for this use case

**Production approach per video:**

**FNOL Video:**
1. Generate Act 1 parking lot clip in Kling
2. Build LiveKit composite web template (phone UI + transcript panel)
3. Configure FNOL dev agent with prompt
4. Craig calls in as Mike, LiveKit records composite
5. Build Act 3 dashboard as HTML/CSS, screen capture
6. Build Act 4 CTA card as HTML/CSS, screen capture
7. Assemble all acts in Descript with music + transitions

**Smart Inbox Video:**
1. Generate Act 1 inbox counter + Act 2 Phase 1 email flow in Kling (macro views)
2. Build Act 2 Phase 2 email detail UI as HTML/CSS (zoom-in: ACORD extraction, gap analysis, draft, AMS integration)
3. Generate or composite Act 2 Phase 3 zoom-out in Kling
4. Build Act 3 stats and Act 4 CTA as HTML/CSS
5. Assemble all in Descript with music + transitions
