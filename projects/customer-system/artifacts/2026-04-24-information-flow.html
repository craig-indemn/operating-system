Where did this Playbook come from?

The entity itself (the schema) was designed today in conversation. There was a stale "Playbook" definition in the DB before (wrong shape — draft/active/archived states, integration_requirements fields). We dropped it and recreated with the aligned shape. Full rationale in 2026-04-24-playbook-entity-vision.md.

The DISCOVERY record's content (what you see above) was seeded by Claude today. Explicitly not imported from Kyle's PLAYBOOK-v2 doc — that doc is CEO-level intent, not a schema. The seed came from: the four-point structure we articulated for what a first-discovery follow-up must do, plus Craig's instinct about the tone ("continuation, not a pitch"), plus the required_entities list derived from our entity model.

Long-term it should hydrate from data. As more discovery calls flow through, patterns emerge — which wedge candidates lead to booked demos, what commitments are typically exchanged, what language Kyle actually uses in his emails. Those patterns refine the Playbook. Today's record is a minimum viable seed so the Artifact Generator has enough spec to render an email. It's not the final version; it's version one.

What changes when we scale this

Same mechanism generates different artifacts at different stages. At DEMO, Playbook's artifact_intent would be "a demo recap with objections addressed, next-step proposed." At PROPOSAL, it becomes "render the Proposal doc from its Phases + linked Opportunities + AssociateTypes." Same inputs (Deal + Touchpoint + intel + Playbook for stage). Only the stage's Playbook record differs, and the artifact that comes out the other end changes accordingly.

The loop closes at PROPOSAL — that's the terminus. The Proposal entity exists, its Phases exist, and the Artifact Generator reads them to render the document we send to the customer.
