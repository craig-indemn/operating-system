Raw notes from Craig's conversations, organized by person. No synthesis — recording only.

## From Kyle — Customer Delivery System

Kyle's document outlines many components:

### Customer Discovery & Research
- Understanding a customer's operations
- Discovery calls, sample transcripts, recordings
- Reporting that they've shared
- Our own observations of their operation
- Web research on their company
- Understanding what integrations they have with various systems
- Mapping into a **disposition map** — understanding volume of business, how their company functions, what the operations are
- Possibly having a system diagram for the customer
- Having a research document that encapsulates all research on a company
- Detailed integration specification on what integrations they use

### Assessment & Gap Identification
- Understand what problems the customer has
- Map problems to our associate model and outcomes model
- For each operation in their business: breakdown of who owns that task, what systems it interacts with, what people it interacts with
- How their business processes map to our solutions, outcomes, and associates
- Identify gaps between what they need and what we can currently provide

### Customer Playbook
- Entire scope of work for what we should provide to the customer
- Checklist of every task that needs to be accomplished
- Who's on point for each task
- Tier classification (1-3)
- Level of effort
- Ties into Linear issues potentially
- A playbook for how we can be successful with that customer

### Associate Playbook
- Scope of work for building each associate
- Tasks and steps needed
- Checklist with assignee
- Level of effort
- Runbook for how it can be accomplished

### Value & ROI Framing
- Tied to business outcomes and discovery assessments
- Tied to the playbook
- Value proposition for each customer
- How much money can we save them
- Maps to each task/associate/outcome being delivered
- Based on current volume and business knowledge
- Based on planned automation within the playbook
- Granular breakdown of:
  - Current value we're giving the customer
  - Potential for future value
  - When we'd see that come to fruition

### Customer Record
- Owner
- Stage
- Associates built for them
- Outcomes being built for them
- Tier
- Next milestone
- Open tasks

### Associate Record
- Ties to delivery checklist / associate playbook
- Required generally for implementing that associate
- Required inputs
- Integration requirements
- Success metrics
- Typical timeline
- Escalation paths

### Outcome Record
- How outcomes map to associates
- Proof points of outcomes
- How we measure value generated from outcomes
- ROI model template for each outcome

## From George — General Principles & Conference

### Conference Preparation
- Identify list of people attending a conference
- Their titles, roles
- Identify opportunities from those attendees
- Outreach: blogs, videos, live associates we can send people to see
- Gain interest before/during the conference

### General Principles for the System
- Customer discovery from meetings and identifying opportunities
- Meeting intelligence organization
- Email automation and organization
- Customer delivery and proposal templates
- Roles and customer leadership/management — who's on point for a customer, how that flows to the rest of the team
- Managing capacity, planning capacity, understanding capacity
- Ingestion from many data sources: Google Drive, Slack, Apollo, Linear
- **Case studies as the starting point** — create a case study for a customer that is everything we will deliver and how it impacts their business, then work backwards on delivering it
- Generating analytical and usage reports
- Generating evaluations over systems/agents/associates including observatory and usage metrics
- Connections to Stripe for monetary impact
- Demos in the system for sales conversations
- Weekly summaries: where we're at with customers, things in motion, things people need awareness of
- Task Kanban board for managing work
- Sales outbound / cold outreach system
- Visual progress indicator on delivery closeness
- Time to completion information
- Intake: transcripts, recordings
- Due dates picked from meetings
- Sending reminders for implementations in Slack
- Breakdown of customers — source of truth
- Customer ROI versus pricing
- Meetings with customers: transcripts, task list, checklist, notes
- Tiers of access
- Person-by-person status of what they're doing
- Metrics: meeting hours, emails per week, time to go live, tasks per person
- How long people have been customers
- Completed tasks for customers vs. action items they've had
