# Business Context

## Company Overview

Indemn builds AI associates for insurance — voice, chat, email — handling workflows humans can't scale. $1.1M ARR, 18 customers, 15-person team. No outside capital driving growth so far.

3.8x MRR growth in 9 months (Jul 2025 $84K annualized → Mar 2026 $320K annualized). 16% avg month-over-month. Top quartile for Series A companies.

Full revenue visibility: $320K SaaS + $780K JM Earnout/RevShare + $96K usage-based converting = $1.2M+ before any new logos or expansions.

## Series A Status

Raising $10M. Challenging math: $10M raise on $1M ARR. Target: $1.5M+ before close.

Framework Venture Partners is the strongest active lead — $10M+ round lead. Olivia Tiessen reviewed financials, asked for pipeline. Also invested in Quandary (renewals workflow) — non-competitive complement.

Primary Venture Partners pitch successful — requested financials, discussing next steps.

Kyle's $10M plan: build the middle market sales/implementation machine + deepen JM + activate 1-2 product programs (umbrella first) + scale channel motion.

Kyle's $25M vision: 3-5 carrier partnerships, 50+ active customers, 2-3 embedded specialty programs, full team and platform.

## Kyle's Three-Tier Model (from "The Proposal")

1. **Middle Market** ($30K-$150K) — "Build the army." AEs land, FDEs implement, CS expands. Volume play. INSURICA, Union General, GIC prove the pattern. $10M mostly funds this.
2. **Enterprise** — "Us, strategically." JM model. Custom architecture, direct attention. 3-5 max simultaneously.
3. **Product + Partnerships** — "Immediate value at scale." Product-led, lower touch. Umbrella insurance = first candidate. 101 Weston channel delivering groups of customers.

The arc: "From company that does AI communications for insurance → company that builds and distributes insurance products through AI at scale."

## The Moat Question

Kyle and Cam agree: "Revenue engines = the moat." But called the articulation "uninspired."

Current best candidate: "We're the only AI that can actually sell an insurance policy."

Real moat (Craig's thesis): the insurance-native domain model + agent-accessible platform + compounding intelligence across every deployment. Every deployment enriches the model. Every integration becomes reusable. The platform IS the moat.

Kyle's intellectual framework (Constraint Removal thought piece): "Insurance's primary constraint has always been ACCESS. Generative AI removes the access constraint entirely." Looking for the "Wikipedia of insurance + AI" — the thing with no predecessor.
