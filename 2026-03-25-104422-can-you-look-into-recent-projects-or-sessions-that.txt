  6. No contact model beyond Customer вҖ” agencies interact with carrier reps,

  - Rule is too generic вҖ” underwriting rules, rating rules, compliance rules,

  - Premium is three different concepts вҖ” quoted premium, written premium,
  earned premium
  - No distinction between personal and commercial lines вҖ” completely different
  data, workflows, carriers, compliance

  insurance, and codebase validation agents. Once all five are back, we can

вҸә Three of five are back and the picture is crystal clear. The MGA/wholesaler

  The Verdict: 22 Entities Was a Skeleton. The Real System Needs ~50-60.

  But that's not the problem вҖ” the problem is structural. The original list was
  flat. The real domain model needs to be organized into sub-domains:

  1. Core Insurance
  Policy, Coverage, Risk/InsuredAsset, Endorsement, PolicyTransaction, Binder,
  Renewal, Claim, Incident, LossHistory, LineOfBusiness, ClassCode

  2. Submission & Quoting
  Submission, SubmissionLine, Quote, QuoteOption, Subjectivity, RatingWorksheet,
   Application, SubmissionRequirement

  3. Parties
  Insured (NOT Customer вҖ” the retail agent is the customer for an MGA, the
  insured is a separate entity), RetailAgent, Agency, Carrier, Underwriter,
  DistributionPartner, Contact/Party (generalized)

  4. Authority & Compliance
  DelegatedAuthority, AuthorityLimit, Referral, License,
  SurplusLinesTransaction, DiligentSearch, UnderwritingDecision, ComplianceAudit

  5. Financial
  Premium (written/earned), Payment, Invoice, PaymentPlan, PremiumTrustAccount,
  CommissionSchedule, CommissionSplit, CommissionStatement,
  RevenueShareAgreement, Bordereaux/CarrierReport

  6. Distribution & Delivery
  Program, ProductConfiguration, Deployment, Embed/DistributionSurface,
  CarrierAgreement, Panel/Market, Appetite

  7. Operations
  Interaction, Task, Diary/Suspense, Correspondence, Document,
  Opportunity/CoverageGap

  8. Platform Layer (separate from domain)
  Associate, Skill, Workflow, Template, Rule, KnowledgeBase, Channel,
  Organization

  Three Critical Insights From the Validation

  Insight 1: "Customer" is wrong as an entity name. In retail agency, Customer =
   the policyholder. In MGA/wholesale, Customer = the retail agent. In embedded,
   Customer = the end consumer. The actual entities are Insured (the
  person/business with the risk) and the various party types (Agent, Agency,
  Carrier, DistributionPartner). The platform's "customer" (the org paying
  Indemn) is the Organization.

  Insight 2: Platform concepts must be separated from domain entities. Workflow,

вҸә Yes, there are. This is core Domain-Driven Design. Let me lay out the
  criteria.

  When Something Should Be Its Own Entity

  An entity is a thing with identity and a lifecycle. There are clear tests:

  1. Does it have its own identity?
  Can you point to "this specific one" and distinguish it from another? A Policy
   has a policy number. A Submission has an ID. You track them individually. вҶ’
  Entity.

  A "territory factor of 1.15 for zone 3" doesn't have meaningful identity on
  its own. It only exists as part of a product's rating configuration. вҶ’ Not an
  entity.

  2. Does it have its own lifecycle?
  Does it get created, change state over time, and eventually end? A Submission
  moves from received вҶ’ processing вҶ’ quoted вҶ’ bound. A Claim gets opened,
  investigated, reserved, settled. вҶ’ Entity.

  A ClassCode (NAICS 524210 = "Insurance Agencies and Brokerages") doesn't
  change state. It's reference data. вҶ’ Reference table, not entity.

  3. Can it exist independently?
  Does it make sense on its own, or only in the context of a parent? A Quote can
   exist independently вҖ” you compare quotes from different carriers. вҶ’ Entity.

  A RatingFactor (territory multiplier = 1.15) only makes sense inside a
  product's rate configuration. вҶ’ Embedded/nested object.

  4. Do multiple other entities reference it?
  If many things point to it, it needs its own identity. A Carrier is referenced
   by Submissions, Quotes, Policies, Agreements, Programs. вҶ’ Entity.

  A SubmissionRequirement (checklist of what's needed) is only referenced by its
   Submission. вҶ’ Embedded.

  5. Does it change independently of its parent?
  If a carrier changes their appetite but nothing else about the carrier
  changes, appetite has independent change behavior. вҶ’ Possibly its own entity.
  If appetite only ever changes as part of updating the product definition, it's
   configuration on the Product.

  6. The CLI test (specific to your vision):
  Would you want a dedicated CLI command group for it? indemn submission list,
  indemn policy get, indemn carrier show вҖ” those make sense as top-level
  commands. Would you want indemn rating-factor list? Probably not. That's
